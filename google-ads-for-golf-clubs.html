// BUILT FOR GOLF

Golf doesn't behave like other industries.

Weather, seasons, memberships and travel radius all change how a campaign should be built. Generic account structures ignore every one of them.

01

Tee Sheet Demand

Campaigns split by what someone is actually booking: a casual round, a group, a lesson or a membership enquiry. Each one deserves its own message and its own bid.

02

Midweek and Off-Peak

Weekends usually sell themselves. The money is in filling Tuesday mornings, so we run offer-led campaigns scheduled against your genuinely quiet windows.

03

Drive-Time Targeting

Golfers travel further than most businesses assume. We target the radius people will genuinely cross for your course, not an arbitrary postcode list.

04

Seasonality Planning

Budget planned against your real demand curve. Peak season carries efficiency, shoulder season is where growth hides, and quiet months get worked rather than written off.

05

Corporate and Group Days

The highest value booking on most tee sheets, and the one most clubs never advertise for. Long sales cycle, different keywords, worth its own campaign.

06

Booking System Tracking

Conversions wired back from your booking platform so you can see which campaigns produced real rounds, not just form fills. This gets checked before anything else.

// COMMON QUESTIONS

Before you ask.

How much should a golf club spend on Google Ads?

It depends on your catchment and what you're selling. A single-course club filling midweek rounds usually needs far less than a golf school selling multi-day programs interstate. We start from your booking value and target volume, then work backwards to a budget, rather than picking a number first.

Does Google Ads work for clubs outside major cities?

Often better. Regional clubs face less competition on cost per click, and golfers routinely travel for a round. The key is targeting the drive-time radius people will actually cross, not just your postcode.

How do you handle golf's seasonality?

We plan budget around your real demand curve rather than spending evenly all year. Peak season carries efficiency, shoulder season is usually where the growth is, and quiet periods get offer-led campaigns aimed at midweek and off-peak slots.

Can you track bookings from our booking system?

Yes. Most golf booking platforms can pass a conversion back to Google Ads, though the setup varies by provider. Tracking is the first thing we check, because campaign decisions are only as good as the data underneath them.
